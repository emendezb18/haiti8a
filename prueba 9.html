HOLIDAY/CELEBRATION

If you one day visit Haiti, you'll want to go to their beautiful carnivals. You need to reserve these days for your end-of-year holidays on February 27, 28 and March 1, 2022 for carnival. This Carnival festival is very loved and very well received by the people of Haiti and not only by these people but also by the tourists. This is Haiti's largest holiday, Carnival, which lasts for several weeks every year starting at the end of January. Fat Tuesday, often known as Mardi Gras, is the culmination of these colorful, exuberant celebrations. In the capital city of Port-au-Prince, Haiti holds its biggest yearly Carnival. This season is characterized by colorful parades, vivacious music, folk art, and vivacious dance performances. The official Carnival celebrations go on for six days in the first month of the year. Haitians love this kind of carnival and festivals throughout the year because Haitian culture combines aspects from Africa, France, and the West Indies. The Creole language, once a social barrier, is now being used to try to define a national culture. The language is employed in some official activities as well as in music, dance, drama, and literature. Voodoo culture uses its own vocabulary.
