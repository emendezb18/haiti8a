MAJOR CITIES/CAPITAL

Haiti has a bunch of beautiful cities around its territory. In first place, obviously the capital of this country, Port-Au-Prince is the most popular and biggest city in Haiti. The city served as a colonial seat for the French in 1770, and it was the city that passed through the independence movement. This city attracts thousands of visitors every year with its beautiful tropical landscape, colorful buildings, and culture. Port-au-Prince has a population of 987,310. It is the most important city in Haiti because it serves as the center of attention for the majority of the government's mission activities as well as the seat of all government and international activity. If you want to visit a modern, wheled, good tourist spot, you need to go to Petion-Ville. It has some good places that many tourists visit, like the richest city in this country, not only to visit the bunch of good restaurants that Petion-Ville has. This city is located in the metropolitan area of Port-au-Prince. It is one of the most up-to-date and future areas of Haiti. Most of the tourist activity is developed in this area of the country. Many diplomats, foreign businessmen, and a large number of wealthy citizens do business and live in Pétion-Ville. Its name was acquired by a Haitian independence leader named Alexandre Sabès Pétion, who fought in Haiti's wars of independence in the early 19th century and later became president of the southern kingdom of Haiti. Now this is a model city. Pétion Ville suffered extensive damage in the 2010 Haiti earthquake.
